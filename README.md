# Risk-Matrix
I have developed a Risk Matrix for possible Insider threat and External Cyber attack.
This risk matrix is designed to assess and visualize potential risks from both insider threats and external cyberattacks by comparing two key factors: the likelihood of an event occurring and the severity of its impact. Different cyber incidents—such as ransomware attacks, phishing attempts, zero-day exploits, and insider data leaks—are mapped into risk levels ranging from Very Low to Extreme. 
The matrix also shows how security controls influence the level of risk. For example, environments with minimal protection, such as no access restrictions or monitoring, are more vulnerable and fall into higher risk categories. In contrast, stronger security measures like firewalls, multi-factor authentication (MFA), SIEM monitoring, user behavior analytics, access controls, and employee awareness training significantly reduce the likelihood and impact of threats. By organizing these scenarios visually, the matrix helps security teams understand which threats require immediate attention, prioritize mitigation strategies, and implement the right combination of technical, physical, and administrative controls to better protect organizational systems and sensitive data.
